# Chamber Behavior Language

Date: 2026-04-08  
Status: Active reference language

This doc translates VisuLive's chamber/world principles into explicit behavior language.

The chamber is not support geometry. It is a stage author, frame author, and spatial instrument.

If a moment matters, the chamber must be able to:

- compress the frame
- open the frame
- cage the frame
- sweep the frame
- collapse the frame
- remember the frame

## Core Rule

The chamber must be capable of owning the image without the hero.

If the hero is removed or minimized, the viewer should still be able to tell:

- what kind of moment this is
- whether the frame is gathering, spending, or recovering
- where the spatial authority is coming from
- whether the room feels wider, heavier, or more enclosed than before

If that is not true, the chamber is still decorative.

## Chamber Roles

The chamber can play these roles:

- `background-scaffold` for quiet holds and low-spend setup
- `framing-architecture` for reveal, gather, release, and haunt
- `event-platform` for rupture, drop, collapse, and strong transition moments

These are not visual presets. They are authority modes.

## Behavior Vocabulary

The chamber should behave with verbs, not vague motion:

- `hold`
- `tighten`
- `cage`
- `open`
- `fan`
- `sweep`
- `rise`
- `collapse`
- `scar`
- `echo`
- `hover`
- `unfold`

## Cue-Family Mapping

### Gather

Before:
- chamber tightens
- negative space narrows
- framing becomes more architectural

During:
- rings align
- beams or halos imply pressure
- the room feels like it is preparing to spend

After:
- either release or rupture becomes legible
- the chamber should not immediately go flat

### Reveal

Before:
- chamber is partially obscured or compressed
- the frame feels withheld

During:
- architecture opens
- fans, apertures, and rings become readable
- the room feels larger and more deliberate

After:
- a new frame posture is established
- the chamber should hold the new scale long enough to be read

### Rupture

Before:
- chamber gathers pressure
- structure becomes taut

During:
- the chamber is allowed to dominate the frame
- scale, sweep, and directional discharge must be decisive
- the hero may be reduced or displaced if the chamber carries the moment better

After:
- the chamber must not instantly reset
- the room should keep some altered state, scar, or echo

### Release

Before:
- chamber should feel over-pressured

During:
- chamber opens
- hard compression relaxes
- the frame exhales rather than spikes

After:
- the chamber keeps a trace of the previous state

### Haunt

Before:
- active motion reduces
- memory becomes the focus

During:
- the chamber holds ghosts, stains, echoes, and delayed structure
- the room should feel inhabited by what happened earlier

After:
- the chamber still looks changed
- the image should not fully forget itself

### Reset

Before:
- chamber is visibly overcommitted or congested

During:
- the frame clears
- the architecture re-reads
- spent structure is reduced

After:
- a new posture is available
- the reset should feel like a real state change, not a small fade

## Image-Class Criteria

The chamber is successful when it can read as one of these classes:

- `Aperture Cage`: enclosed, tense, compressed, forward pressure
- `Cathedral Fan`: wide, open, architectural, directional
- `Collapse Well`: falling inward, darkening, structural loss of stability
- `Ghost Chamber`: lingering, haunted, memory-heavy, delayed
- `Field Bloom`: spacious, expanded, atmospheric, soft but still authored

## Anti-Goals

The chamber must not become:

- decorative orbit lines around the hero
- passive background fog
- a repeated ring belt with no authority change
- bright filler that only exists to make the hero look more expensive
- motion without spatial meaning

## Readable Test Questions

Use these questions during review:

- Can the chamber own the frame without the hero?
- Does the chamber read differently for gather, reveal, rupture, release, haunt, and reset?
- Does the room feel wider, heavier, more enclosed, or more open when it should?
- Does the chamber keep a memory of rupture or release instead of snapping back immediately?
- Is the chamber carrying actual authority, or just supporting the hero?
