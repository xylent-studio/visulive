# VisuLive Graduation Rubric

Date: 2026-04-22
Status: Active graduation system

This document defines exactly what `lab`, `frontier`, `flagship`, and
`retired` mean for anthology families.

It is the advancement contract for future repertoire work.

## Status Definitions

### `lab`

`lab` means:

- experimental or early family
- not required in default untouched runs
- may exist as doctrine, metadata, prototype behavior, or narrow frontier-only work
- must still declare purpose, absence rules, and proof requirements before landing

### `frontier`

`frontier` means:

- eligible for frontier no-touch runs
- coherent enough to appear without manual rescue
- must already show quiet, impact, and aftermath value
- must already have a plausible safe-tier posture
- may still be gated away from the default flagship mix

### `flagship`

`flagship` means:

- allowed in the default public untouched show
- safe-tier readable and proof-backed
- strong enough to trust in front of users
- distinct enough that it increases repertoire instead of pretending to
  diversify it

### `retired`

`retired` means:

- rejected
- merged into another family
- or superseded by a stronger answer

Retirement is allowed.
Keeping weak families alive out of sentiment is not.

## Common Graduation Requirements

Every family, regardless of category, needs:

- a named cue role
- quiet behavior
- impact behavior
- aftermath behavior
- absence rules
- a safe-tier expectation
- at least one no-touch proof target
- explicit runtime ownership or a documented blocking owner

## Family-Specific Rubrics

### Hero Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: a hero state stays readable without full-shell fill light
- impact: at least one hero mutation or alternate species changes image class
- aftermath: hero can demote, scar, ghost, or disappear after a cue
- safe-tier: seam, cavity, and silhouette survive on `webgpu / safe`
- no-touch: hero leadership appears without monopolizing the whole run

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- hero no longer crowds world/chamber authority by default
- alternate hero behavior is readable from across the room
- suppression or world-as-hero is visibly possible

### World Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: world-led frames remain active in low-energy passages
- impact: at least three mutation verbs visibly change the frame
- aftermath: changed space, stain, or altered chamber state remains visible
- safe-tier: world authority survives without premium-only post
- no-touch: world-led frames appear in untouched runs without manual forcing

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- world takeover feels authored instead of rare or accidental
- mutation reads as changed space, not preset swapping

### Consequence / Aftermath Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: baseline stays selective and mostly clean
- impact: cues visibly alter the frame, not just brightness
- aftermath: at least two aftermath states remain distinct
- safe-tier: consequence remains readable on `webgpu / safe`
- no-touch: consequences happen in untouched runs without becoming permanent treatment

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- consequence is clearly earned
- aftermath stays visible long enough to matter, then clears
- no-touch proof shows consequence without flat over-processing

### Lighting / Cinematography Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: light and framing can carry tension without a large event
- impact: at least two light-rig or camera-phrase states read differently
- aftermath: recovery framing differs from impact framing
- safe-tier: silhouette and shot-class survive trimmed budgets
- no-touch: light/camera phrasing helps define cue identity automatically

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- lighting and framing read as co-authors, not generic support
- room-scale readability stays strong across quiet and impact passages

### Particle / Field Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: particles behave like weather, residue, or atmosphere instead of chatter
- impact: at least one cue lets particles become the readable authority
- aftermath: field residue differs from impact spray
- safe-tier: fewer particles still preserve the field role
- no-touch: role-based fields appear without steering

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- field roles are distinct and repeatable
- particles stop reading as decorative support

### Mixed Media / Compositor Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: absence remains the default unless a family is clearly needed
- impact: compositor work changes image consequence or memory without collage behavior
- aftermath: overlays, scars, or reveals behave like authored language
- safe-tier: the family remains legible without premium-only multipass tricks
- no-touch: usage appears only when the cue or memory state earns it

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- the family reads like VisuLive, not pasted-in media
- the family improves consequence, memory, or identity in untouched runs

### Motif / Memory Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: recurrence can appear without feeling repetitive
- impact: a cue can plant a recognizable motif
- aftermath: the motif can return altered later
- safe-tier: recurrence is still legible from distance
- no-touch: untouched runs visibly remember earlier events

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- the show remembers itself over minutes, not just frames
- recurrence deepens authorship instead of producing sameness

### Music Semantics Families

To move `lab -> frontier`:

- quiet: silence and room-floor states are distinctly interpreted
- impact: similar energy with different musical character yields different cue classes
- aftermath: release and recovery timing are musically motivated
- safe-tier: semantic differences stay visible without premium-only spectacle
- no-touch: the autonomous run becomes more legible without operator help

To move `frontier -> flagship`:

- cue hierarchy consistently avoids one-house-style collapse
- no-touch sessions show deeper musical interpretation over time

## Retirement Conditions

Retire a family when:

- it fails to create a distinct image class
- it cannot survive safe tier without becoming flat or noisy
- it duplicates a stronger family
- it needs constant intervention to look good
- it only exists as metadata and never becomes visible

## Evidence Rule

A family cannot graduate on theory alone.

Graduation should cite:

- the relevant proof-pack or analyzer evidence
- the review questions it now clears
- the current runtime owner or completed extraction

If those three things are missing, the family is not ready.
